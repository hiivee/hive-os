# Your agent's memory · in plain words

Your Hive OS comes with four kinds of memory. You do not set them up by hand: during
onboarding (`START.md`) the agent installs them for you and shows each one working.

## The four brains

1. **It remembers you.** The facts you tell it (who you are, your clients, your goals) get
   written down and loaded every session. You never repeat yourself.

2. **It remembers the work.** Everything you build together is kept and searchable by
   meaning, so next week the agent picks up where you left off instead of from zero.

3. **It finds things fast.** Ask "where is the part that does X" and it goes straight to it
   in your code or your notes, by meaning, not by guessing filenames.

4. **It connects the dots.** It can answer "what links this and that" across all your
   documents and projects, because it keeps a map of how your things relate.

## What this means for you

The agent stops being a blank slate every time. It gets to know your world and keeps it.
That is the difference between a chatbot and a system that works for you over months.

## Keep it fresh

When we improve the engine, you inherit it: just say "update my Hive OS" and the agent
runs `hive sync`. Your private notes never move. The memory only gets sharper.

## Deeper (optional)

There is a fifth, advanced brain (a full knowledge graph over everything you feed it) that
needs your own AI key. It is off by default. Turn it on when you want the deepest memory.
See `harness/memory/README.md` for the technical view.
