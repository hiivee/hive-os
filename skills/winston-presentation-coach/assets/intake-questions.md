# Intake Question Bank

Use only the questions that match the user's mode. Ask in a single message — do not drip questions one at a time, that wastes the user's turn.

Phrase questions conversationally. Group related questions. Provide examples in parentheses where the answer space is non-obvious.

---

## Universal questions (ask in every mode)

1. **Topic** — What is the talk about, in one sentence?
2. **Audience** — Who is in the room? (job titles, expertise level, what they already believe about the topic)
3. **Desired outcome** — What do you want them to do, decide, or believe by the time they leave? (Winston's "vision" hinges on this.)
4. **Setting and time** — Conference / boardroom / Zoom / classroom? How long is the slot? How many slides do you currently have or plan to have?

---

## Mode-specific add-ons

### Mode A — Opening
Add:
- 5. **The single most valuable thing the audience will walk away knowing.** If you could only deliver one outcome, what is it? (This becomes the empowerment promise.)
- 6. **Why now?** Why does this matter today, this quarter, this year? (Used in the "context" portion of the first 60 seconds.)

### Mode B — Memorability
Add:
- 5. **The one idea you want them to remember six months later.** Just one — Winston's Salient Idea is singular, never plural.
- 6. **Any existing branding** — Do you already have a name, tagline, image, or hand-gesture associated with this idea? (We will reuse rather than invent if so.)
- 7. **The counterintuitive part** — What does your audience currently believe that your idea contradicts? (This becomes the Surprise.)

### Mode C — Structure
Add:
- 5. **Goal of the talk** — Inform (lecture-style) or expose (job talk / pitch / conference talk)? Winston treats these differently — exposition talks must establish vision and credibility in the first 5 minutes; lecture-style talks have more room to breathe.
- 6. **Specific proof points** — What concrete steps, results, or artefacts can you point to as evidence you have done something real? (List them as bullet points; we will sort and trim.)
- 7. **Contributions** — In one or two phrases each, what are the discrete things you are claiming credit for?

### Mode D — Slides
Add:
- 5. **Share the deck or describe it.** If sharing is impossible, describe slide by slide: title, words on slide, font size if known, any images or logos.
- 6. **Slide count** vs. **time slot** — used to flag the "too many slides" crime (Winston's rule of thumb: roughly one slide per minute is already on the high side).
- 7. **Existing final slide** — what is on it right now? (This is almost always the slide that needs the deepest rework.)

### Mode E — Full build
Run **all** universal + all four mode add-ons. This is a heavier intake — warn the user up front: "This will take 2–3 minutes to fill out, but it is the difference between a generic talk and a Winston-grade one."

---

## Intake hygiene

- If the user has already answered a question earlier in the conversation, do not re-ask it. Acknowledge what you already know and ask only the gaps.
- If the user gives a vague answer ("technical audience"), push once for specificity ("which roles — engineers, architects, CTOs?"). Then proceed.
- If the user refuses or cannot answer a question, proceed with a clearly-marked assumption and flag it in the deliverable so they can correct it.
